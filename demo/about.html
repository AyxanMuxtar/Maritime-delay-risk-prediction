About the project

Caspian Maritime Delay-Risk Forecast

A daily decision-support dashboard for estimating weather-driven maritime delay risk across major Caspian ports.

What this product does

Maritime operations can be disrupted by strong wind, dangerous gusts, heavy precipitation, fog, snowfall, and rough sea conditions. This dashboard turns those weather and sea-state signals into readable risk levels for Baku, Aktau, Anzali, Turkmenbashi, and Makhachkala.

The goal is not to predict confirmed port closures. Instead, the system gives planners an early warning signal: which port-days deserve normal operation, monitoring, caution, manual review, or postponement.

Coverage

Five Caspian ports

Baku, Aktau, Anzali, Makhachkala, and Turkmenbashi.

Forecast window

Rolling 30 days

The calendar updates daily and always shows the next operating window.

Output

Risk + action

Each day is shown as Low, Moderate, High, Very High, or Severe with a recommended response.

How it works

Data and pipeline

Weather data comes from Open-Meteo and is stored in a DuckDB pipeline with raw, staging, and analytics layers. The pipeline handles missing values, visibility coverage limits, outlier flags, date checks, and automated quality gates.

Features

The system builds seasonal, rolling, lag, anomaly, visibility, and wave-proxy features. Historical wave height is estimated from wind and port exposure, giving the project a maritime-specific signal beyond a normal weather forecast.

Model and forecast

The selected production model is chosen in the Day 8 modeling workflow, then retrained by the pipeline on the latest analytics data. Forecast-based days use live weather inputs; later dates fall back to smoothed city/day-of-year climatology.

What counts as a risky day

A day becomes risky when at least one maritime threshold is breached: strong sustained wind, dangerous gusts, heavy precipitation, snowfall, low visibility/fog, or high wave conditions. The website shows both the final risk estimate and the key conditions behind it when a day is clicked.

Limitations

The forecast is a planning signal, not a guarantee of actual delay. Visibility data is only directly available from 2022 onward, older visibility rows are handled through imputation and flags, and historical sea-state is estimated with a wave proxy rather than direct measured wave observations.
